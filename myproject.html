My Projects

This is the work on simulating brain activities using Competitive Queuing Neural Networks. Developed during my time with the Tujenge Scholars Program, this project emulates the brain's self-excitatory and self-inhibitory mechanisms. Using Octave, a powerful language for matrix operations, we constructed a network with sequence, competitive, and output layers. Our simulation closely mirrored recorded chimpanzee brain activity, showcasing different processing speeds for various tasks. This research unveils the potential of Competitive Queuing Neural Networks in modeling cognitive processes. I am really grateful for the Tujenge Scholars Program for enabling this exploration..
